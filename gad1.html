General Anxiety Disorder

View original source here

Anxiety is a feeling of unease, such as worry or fear, that can be mild or severe.
The information in this section is about a specific condition called generalised anxiety disorder (GAD). GAD is a long-term condition that causes you to feel anxious about a wide range of situations and issues, rather than 1 specific event.

People with GAD feel apprehensive most days and often battle to recall the last time they felt carefree. As soon as one jittery thought is concluded, another may appear about a different issue. Everyman has temperaments of anxiety at some point in their life. For illustration, you may feel distressed and anxious about sitting an exam, or having a medical test or job interview. During times like these, feeling anxious can be wholly healthy. But some people find it hard to dominate their worries. Their feelings of misery are more steady and can often affect their daily lives.

Anxiety is the main syndrome of several circumstances, including:

  • Panic disorder
  • Phobias, such as agoraphobia or claustrophobia
  • Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD)
  • Social anxiety disorder (social phobia)
